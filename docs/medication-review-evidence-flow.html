MedTracker learning guide

How medicine-label evidence becomes a practitioner review record

A visual map of what the app knows, what it does not know, and where a qualified practitioner remains responsible for judgement.

MedTracker organises public label evidence for a conversation. It does not decide whether a medicine combination is appropriate and does not tell someone to change treatment.

The evidence journey

01

Public label

MedTracker imports the Drug Interactions section from an openFDA or DailyMed SPL record into the local database.

02

Unreviewed evidence

The raw narrative begins as unknown risk, unknown match confidence, and unreviewed. It cannot create a user prompt.

03

Pair review gate

A human reviewer identifies the two medicine terms, assigns a source-risk level, and records match confidence.

04

Practitioner discussion

An adult takes the source evidence to a pharmacist, nurse, GP, or prescriber. The app does not choose the outcome.

05

Review record

MedTracker records who was consulted, their role, the date, the outcome, and a note for the next appointment PDF.

Two separate questions

Source risk

What does the reviewed source evidence describe?

  • Low, moderate, high, or unknown
  • Unknown means unclassified, not most dangerous
  • This is not a diagnosis or treatment recommendation
Match confidence

How sure are we that this evidence matches these medicines?

  • Low, moderate, high, or unknown
  • Low-confidence items are filtered by default
  • The user still sees how many items were filtered

What each status means

Needs review

The evidence has not yet been discussed with a practitioner.

Reviewed

A practitioner discussion happened and the details were recorded.

Expected as prescribed

The practitioner confirmed that the combination was expected in this person's treatment.

Not relevant

The evidence does not apply to this person's actual medicine use.

Filtered

A low-risk or low-confidence item is hidden from the default view to reduce noise.
